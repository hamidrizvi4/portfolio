    Favorite opening — Sicilian, Najdorf Variation

    The most-played defense to 1. e4 at the elite level. Black plays …a6 on move five — a quiet pawn push that doesn't develop a piece, doesn't capture anything, doesn't attack.

    But it sets up everything. It controls b5, prepares …e5 or …e6, and refuses to commit to a bishop placement until White shows their hand. It's the chess equivalent of shipping the simplest possible interface and letting requirements emerge from contact with users.
